_(AL-tha-ree-on the Third)_

## General Description

- **Age:** Mid-40s during the Great War
- **Appearance:** Tall and regal, with dark brown hair streaked with silver and piercing emerald eyes. Wears ceremonial robes adorned with the royal crest over practical armor in battle. Carries a ceremonial sword passed down through generations.
- **Personality:**

    - **Wise & Compassionate:** Known for his fairness, empathy, and ability to balance mercy with justice.

    - **Strategic & Diplomatic:** Skilled at managing both internal politics and inter-kingdom alliances.

    - **Charismatic Leader:** Inspires loyalty from both royal forces and common citizens.

    - **Patient & Resolute:** Calm under pressure, especially in war; willing to make personal sacrifices for the greater good.

## Skills & Abilities

- **Swordsmanship:** Trained in the ways of combat but rarely fights on the frontlines; relies on his personal guards and Pillars in battle.
- **Leadership:** Exceptional in rallying troops, managing kingdom-wide strategy, and negotiating alliances.
- **Tactical Insight:** Capable of identifying enemy weaknesses and coordinating multi-faction campaigns.
- **Charisma & Influence:** Able to sway nobles, soldiers, and foreign leaders through diplomacy and reason.

## Role in the Great War

- **Alliance Builder:** Worked tirelessly to unite the royal and noble factions of Aurelion, and later coordinated with the Ashkara Pridelands and the Sanctum of Solythea.
- **King in Battle:** Delegated battlefield command to the Pillars but personally oversaw strategy, logistics, and morale.
- **Protector of the Kingdom:** Balanced internal politics with wartime pressures, ensuring the royal family remained a stabilizing force.
- **Post-War Leadership:** Initiated reforms and checks and balances to prevent future corruption; led Aurelion into a golden age of peace.

## Relationship to Others

- **Royal Family:** Deeply cares for his wife, Queen Lyriana, and his children—Prince Caelum and Princesses Elowen and Mireya. Provides guidance, training oversight, and mentorship.
- **The Hero:** Trusted advisor and ally during the Great War; coordinated strategic support and battlefield integration with the hero’s efforts.
- **The Five Pillars:** Relied heavily on them; each Pillar was both a battlefield asset and a personal friend.
