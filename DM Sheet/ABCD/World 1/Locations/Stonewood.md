# Stonewood

A walled trade city situated at the edge of the Evergreen Forest, serving as the main hub between the surrounding towns and villages. Large enough to have a permanent Church presence, a busy market, and a professional city guard, but small enough that most long-time residents know each other by face if not by name.

## üïØÔ∏è PRESENT

_20 years after the Demon King's return_
Stonewood's walls still stand. Its market still runs. The Church bells still ring on schedule. To a traveler passing through, it might seem like a city carrying on ‚Äî resilient, even admirable. But spend a night here and something else becomes clear. The laughter is too quiet. The market stalls close earlier than they used to. People don't linger in the streets after dark. The Church is full not because of faith but because people have nowhere else to put their fear.
The war with the Demon King has not touched Stonewood's walls directly, but it has touched everything inside them. Refugees from smaller destroyed villages have swelled the population. Food is rationed quietly ‚Äî not by law yet, but by the invisible hand of scarcity. The city guard has tripled in size but halved in morale. Crime has risen in the alleys the guard doesn't bother to patrol anymore. The Church preaches hope but the pews feel more like a shelter than a sanctuary. Stonewood is a city holding its breath.

## üåÑ PAST

_The era of peace following the Demon King's defeat_
Stonewood is a city that feels like a deep breath. The war is over, the Hero won, and the world is rebuilding. Trade flows freely through Stonewood's gates ‚Äî merchants from distant towns, traveling craftsmen, pilgrims visiting the Church to give thanks. The market is loud and colorful. Children run between stalls. The guard is bored in the best possible way. The Church is well-attended out of genuine gratitude rather than desperation. The walls exist more as tradition than necessity ‚Äî the gates are rarely fully closed. The city feels open, warm, and unhurried.

## The Gate & Walls

_==Present==:_ The walls are a patchwork of old and new stonework ‚Äî the newer sections darker, rougher, clearly added in haste during the early years of the war. Watchtowers have been installed at regular intervals along the battlements. The gatehouse is staffed by six guards at minimum with a sergeant present at all times. Travelers are questioned and bags are checked. Refugees are funneled to a separate processing area outside the eastern gate where they can wait days for entry. A notice board at the gate lists contraband items ‚Äî the list has grown long.
_==Past==:_ The city walls are old but well-maintained ‚Äî a point of civic pride. The gatehouse is staffed by two guards at most, and familiar faces are waved through with a nod. Merchants shout greetings as they pass. The gates close at sundown more out of habit than necessity. The whole structure feels more like a landmark than a fortification.

## The Church of the Goddess

_==Present==:_ The largest building in the city, built from pale stone that glows faintly in the morning light. Inside, the pews are packed at all hours ‚Äî families sleeping in the aisles, refugees huddled in side chapels. The Bishop delivers loud, urgent sermons about divine protection and endurance. The Church has become the city's de facto refugee processing center, food distribution point, and grief counseling office all at once. The staff is stretched thin and exhausted. The doors never close because there are always people who have nowhere else to go. The atmosphere is heavy ‚Äî desperate prayers, quiet weeping, the smell of too many people in too small a space.
_==Past==:_ The largest building in the city, built from pale stone that catches the morning light beautifully. The interior is open and airy with tall windows and wooden pews worn smooth from decades of use. Services are joyful community events ‚Äî singing, shared meals afterward, a genuine sense of gratitude for the peace the world has won. The resident Priest, Father Cedric, knows every family in the city by name. The doors are open from dawn to dusk and the atmosphere inside is warm, quiet, and welcoming.

## The Market District

_==Present==:_ A wide cobblestone square lined with stalls and surrounded by shop fronts. Half the original vendors have been replaced by refugees selling whatever they managed to carry out of their destroyed homes ‚Äî secondhand tools, worn clothing, small personal items. Exotic goods are nearly gone. What remains is practical: food, basic supplies, cheap medicine. Prices have crept up on everything and nobody pretends otherwise. The atmosphere is transactional and tense. Vendors watch their stock carefully. Pickpocketing is common enough that most people keep a hand on their coin purse. The market closes an hour before sunset and the square empties fast ‚Äî nobody wants to be caught outside after dark.
_==Past==:_ A wide cobblestone square that is the beating heart of the city. Loud, colorful, and fragrant ‚Äî spices from the south, bolts of cloth from the east, fresh produce from surrounding farms piled high on wooden carts. Street performers work the crowd. Vendors argue cheerfully with each other over shared corners. Children chase each other between the legs of adults. The smell of roasting meat drifts from a corner stall that always has a line. The market runs from sunrise to an hour before sunset and the square stays lively long after it officially closes.

## Thornside Alley

_Location:_ A maze of narrow streets and cramped courtyards behind the market district, sandwiched between the backs of merchant buildings and the city's inner wall.
_==Present==:_ Thornside is now a dense, self-contained district that the city guard has quietly abandoned. Refugees have built lean-tos and partition walls between existing buildings, turning alleyways into corridors and courtyards into common rooms. Unlicensed vendors operate openly. There are at least two underground fighting rings, an unlicensed apothecary rumored to sell Church-banned substances, and an information brokering network operating out of a former cooperage at the far end of the main lane. It is not openly dangerous if you carry yourself right, but it is not safe either.
